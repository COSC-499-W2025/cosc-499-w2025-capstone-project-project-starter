## 1. Team Vision and Goals

We strive to function as a team that:
- Values all members as people, acknowledging diverse backgrounds, strengths, and challenges.
- Supports continuous learning, growth, and improvement for every member.
- Prioritizes high-quality project outcomes through shared responsibility, accountability, and respectful collaboration.
- Maintains honesty, transparency, and professionalism in all team interactions.
- Creates a positive environment where all members feel valued, heard, and empowered to contribute.

## 2. Expectations

### A. Meetings
- Meetings will be held on: Wednesday’s 9:30AM-11:00AM
- Members are expected to arrive on time and be prepared.
- Absences must be communicated at least 24 hours in advance.
- Meeting agendas will be shared by: Whoever wants to.
- Decisions from meetings will be documented and shared with the team.

### B. Communication & Collaboration
- Primary communication platform(s): Discord or other SNS
- Response time expectation: Depends on urgency
- All communication must be respectful, constructive, and professional.
- Members will actively listen, encourage input, and avoid interrupting or dismissing others.
- Collaboration will follow a transparent process where tasks, updates, and decisions are visible to all members.

### C. Distribution & Delivery of Work
- Tasks will be assigned based on availability, strengths, and skill-building goals.
- Every member is responsible for completing their work by agreed-upon deadlines.
- If issues arise, members must notify the team early and ask for support.
- Submitted work should meet team quality standards and follow project requirements.
- Members will review one another’s contributions respectfully and collaboratively.

## 3. Commitment to Appropriate Behavior

- All team members agree to maintain a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free working environment.
- No member will engage in discrimination, intimidation, bullying, or any behavior that disrupts team functioning.
- Conflicts will be addressed professionally and privately with the intention to resolve rather than escalate.

## 4. Additional Ground Rules

(Teams can add, remove, or modify these.)
- Phones and computers will only be used for work during meetings.
- Team members will avoid last-minute work unless unavoidable.
- All major decisions require a majority vote.
- Feedback will be direct, constructive, and received without hostility.

## 5. Conflict Resolution Strategy

Before invoking the firing clause, the team agrees to follow these steps:
1. **Identify the issue**  
   The team discusses specific concerns with the member involved. Clear examples and expectations for improvement are provided.
2. **Direct conversation**  
   Two or more team members meet with the individual privately. The concerns and needed changes are clearly outlined.
3. **Written improvement plan**  
   The team and the member create a short written agreement detailing required behavior or work improvements, plus a timeline.
4. **Check-in meeting**  
   After the agreed timeframe, the team evaluates whether improvements have been made.
5. **Final warning**  
   If no improvement occurs, the team gives a formal final warning and documents it.
6. **Execution of the firing clause**  
   Only after all steps have been followed can the team decide—by vote and instructor approval—to remove the member from the group, following course policy.
